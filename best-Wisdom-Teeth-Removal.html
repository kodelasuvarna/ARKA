    What is Wisdom Tooth Removal?

    Wisdom teeth, often the third molars, are the last of the molars completing the set of 3 molars on each side of the upper and lower arch. In most cases, the third molars erupt without any problem but in some cases, they impinge on the gums. on the gums, or are accompanied by the cyst. At times, they may be misaligned and cause damage to the adjacent tooth. In such cases, the wisdom tooth has to be extracted. The process of removal of wisdom tooth is known as wisdom tooth removal which is undertaken by the oral surgeon.

    When Should you go for Wisdom Tooth Removal?

    Since the wisdom teeth erupt later in life, they need space in the arch. While some people have enough space in their arches to accommodate their wisdom teeth, others may experience problems due to lack of space or improper alignment.     The Procedure of Wisdom Tooth Removal

    Pulpectomy is one of the most common procedures done in the primary dentition i.e the deciduous teeth or the milk teeth. It is a common treatment for teeth that have been very badly damaged by inferior or serious physical or emotional harm. Pulpectomy is a teeth-related procedure that involves the removal of the pulp tissue from the inside of a tooth. The pulp is the soft tissue found in the centre of a tooth that contains blood tubes, nerves, and connective tissue. When the pulp becomes swollen or infected, it can cause extreme pain and lead to serious teeth-related problems.

    • Numbing the area: Before the procedure begins, the dentist will use a local anaesthetic to numb the area around the tooth to be removed. In some cases, they may also offer sedation or general anaesthesia to help you relax during the procedure.
    • Making an incision: The dentist will make a suitable incision in the gum tissue to expose the tooth and bone.
    • Removing the tooth: Using instruments such as the periosteal elevator or dental forceps, the dentist or oral surgeon will carefully remove the tooth from its socket in the bone.
    • Cleaning the area: After the tooth has been removed, the dentist or oral surgeon will clean the area with betadine or saline solution and remove any remaining debris or bacteria.
    • Stitches and packing: In some cases, the dentist or oral surgeon may need to place stitches to close the incision. They will also place a cotton gauze soaked in betadine over the extraction site to help control bleeding and promote healing.
